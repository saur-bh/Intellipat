The Challenge: Why Indian Contextualization is Vital

Directly applying Western assessment tools or relying on outdated Indian scales for ADL skills can lead to inaccurate evaluations. Understanding India's unique context is crucial.

Vineland-3 Adaptation Framework

A hybrid approach is needed: leveraging Vineland-3's robust structure while deeply embedding Indian cultural realities for accurate and meaningful ADL assessment. (Report 1.3)

ADL Milestones & Assessment Focus

Adaptive skills develop with age. Assessment must be tailored to developmental stages and cultural expectations within India. (Report 4.2)

3-6 Years (Preschool)

Focus: Basic ADLs, early socialization, communication.

Key Milestones: Eats with spoon/hands, uses 3-word sentences, simple pretend play. (Report 4.2.1)

7-11 Years (Primary School)

Focus: Expanded self-care, school behavior, home chores.

Key Milestones: Grooms self, performs routine chores, reads age-appropriate material. (Report 4.2.2)

12-19 Years (Teens)

Focus: Independence, responsibility, money skills, safety.

Key Milestones: Manages personal hygiene, prepares simple meals, navigates community. (Report 4.2.3)

Key Factors Influencing ADL in India

A child's adaptive behavior is shaped by a complex interplay of their environment and opportunities. (Report 2.1, 4.3)

Socio-Economic Status (SES)

SES impacts access to nutrition, healthcare, and education, influencing ADL development. (Report 2.1)

Illustrative data showing potential disparity.

Rural vs. Urban Divide

Daily life, responsibilities, and skill expectations vary significantly. (Report 2.1, 4.3)

Rural Context:

  • Often more community involvement.
  • Tasks related to agriculture/local crafts.
  • Different access to formal education/tech.

Urban Context:

  • Greater exposure to diverse services.
  • Technology use more prevalent.
  • Different safety/navigation challenges.

Glimpse into Activity Recommendations

Culturally relevant activities are key to fostering adaptive skills within the child's natural environment. (Report 5.2)

Dressing Practice

"Practice wearing school uniform independently." (Age 3-11)

Kitchen Help

"Make simple snacks like poha or bread-butter." (Age 7-19)

Social Skills

"Greeting family guests with 'namaste'." (Age 3-11)

Reassessment & Progress

Regular reassessment is vital to track progress and adapt support strategies. (Report 6.1)

General Reassessment Timelines:

  • High Support Needed (0-40% score): Reassess in 4 weeks.
  • Moderate Support (41-70%): Reassess in 6 weeks.
  • Improving Skills (71-90%): Reassess in 8-12 weeks.
